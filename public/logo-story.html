An open number, anchored to identity.

The OpenNum mark is an open O and a connected N. The shape is not decorative: it describes how a human-readable inscription number becomes a verifiable Bitcoin identity.

Open

The O stays open.

OpenNum is not a closed account system. The open circle represents an application-layer protocol that any wallet, marketplace, or agent can resolve.

Number

The N is the route.

The N stands for number: the simple inscription number people can remember, share, search, and use as a public identity entrance.

Anchor

Four points, one identity.

The four copper points bind the story together: inscription number, inscription asset, wallet signature, and public profile.

Resolve

From symbol to address.

Behind the mark is the OpenNum resolver: a number maps to current ownership, profile, and payment routing without changing Bitcoin itself.

OpenNum turns an inscription number into a Bitcoin identity. The logo follows the same logic: the O is open, the N is connected, and the four shared endpoints show that the number is not floating above the chain. It is anchored to an inscription, proven by a wallet, and resolved into a public profile.

In short: one open number becomes a verifiable place to find, pay, and recognize someone on Bitcoin.
